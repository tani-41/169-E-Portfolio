I didn't fully understand what engineering was until Grade 12, when I took an electronics course at 17. Before that, I hadn't considered engineering seriously, but that class opened my eyes to coding and circuitry. The process of creating was fun, and the results were rewarding.

Motivated by this passion, I pursued competitions, joined a prebuild team for the UBC Physics Olympics, and secured a competitive internship at an electrical/mechanical firm. At UBC, I continue to grow, notably serving as electrical actuator project lead for the Marine Robotics Design Team.

My goal is to advance technology in ways that are safe, sustainable, and impactful. Outside of engineering, I enjoy music, game development, and exploring electronics.
